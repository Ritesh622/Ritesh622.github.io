Further Reading

This is a small collection of things I have been using while learning and exploring. It is not meant to be complete — just a running list of what I find useful along the way.

Learning Resources

I usually start with Coursera when I don’t know much about a topic. The structure helps me stay on track.

MIT OpenCourseWare is something I use when I want more detailed lectures. It can feel heavy, but it's solid.

I have used NPTEL quite a bit during college. It aligns well with coursework.

I came across the Flower documentation while trying to understand federated learning. Still exploring it.

Sometimes I watch talks from the Simons Institute. I don’t always understand everything, but it’s useful exposure.

Books and Notes

Project Gutenberg is where I go for non-technical reading.

Archive.org has been useful for finding older material.

Open Library is helpful when I just want to quickly check a book.

I’ve occasionally looked at notes from AMS, though I’m still getting comfortable with them.

ILovePDF is something I use often to clean and compress PDFs.
