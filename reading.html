Further Reading

This is a small collection of things I have been using while trying to learn and explore. Nothing here is “complete” — I just keep adding whatever I find useful along the way.

Learning Resources

  • I usually start with Coursera when I don’t know much about a topic. The structure helps me not get lost.
  • MIT OpenCourseWare is something I go to when I want proper lectures. It can feel a bit heavy, but it's solid.
  • I’ve used NPTEL quite a bit during college. It matches well with what we study.
  • I came across the Flower documentation while trying to understand federated learning. Still figuring it out, but it seems practical.
  • Sometimes I watch talks from the Simons Institute. I donot always understand everything, but it is good exposure.

Books and Notes

  • Project Gutenberg is where I go if I want to read something non-technical once in a while.
  • Archive.org has been useful for finding older material that’s otherwise hard to get.
  • Open Library is helpful when I just want to quickly check a book without downloading anything.
  • I’ve occasionally looked at notes from AMS, though I’m still getting comfortable with them.

Tools I Use

  • ILovePDF is something I use a lot to clean up PDFs and reduce file size.

Reading Papers

  • This guide on how to read a paper helped me understand how to even start reading research papers.
  • I also found this Stanford guide useful when I felt completely lost reading papers.

Writing

  • The Chicago Manual of Style is something I refer to when I want to improve clarity.
  • I tried reading “How to Write Mathematics” by Paul Halmos: link. It’s a bit dense, but useful.
  • I like going through Paul Graham’s essays for simple and clear writing.

Further Reading

  • Terence Tao’s blog — I do not understand most of it yet, but it is inspiring.
  • Cal Newport — I read this for ideas on focus and studying better.
  • LessWrong — interesting thoughts on reasoning, though I only read occasionally.
  • Paul Graham essays — I keep coming back to these from time to time.
