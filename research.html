Research

My research focuses on communication-efficient learning, distributed inference, and signal processing, with applications spanning federated systems, wireless communication, and imaging pipelines. I am particularly interested in understanding the trade-offs between statistical efficiency, communication constraints, and practical system design.

Camera and Image Quality Engineering

As part of my work at Qualcomm, I focus on the evaluation and optimization of camera systems, with particular emphasis on image and video quality. This involves analyzing complex camera pipelines and improving their performance across diverse real-world scenarios. My work includes designing tools for systematic image quality assessment using MATLAB and Python, enabling efficient debugging and evaluation.

A key objective is to bridge the gap between algorithmic design and real-world deployment, ensuring that imaging systems perform robustly under varying conditions. This includes improving color reproduction, noise characteristics, and perceptual quality while maintaining computational efficiency and real-time constraints.

Distributed Inference and Learning under Communication Constraints

My primary research during my Ph.D. focuses on developing communication-efficient algorithms for distributed systems, particularly in federated learning settings. In large-scale networks, communication often becomes a bottleneck, and my work studies the trade-off between estimation accuracy and communication cost.

I investigate distributed statistical inference problems where multiple clients collaboratively compute global quantities under strict communication constraints. This includes designing quantization schemes and compression techniques that reduce communication overhead while preserving statistical efficiency. My work also explores structured representations such as type-based quantization for discrete distributions, combining tools from information theory, optimization, and machine learning.

RIS-Assisted NOMA/OMA Communication Systems

I have also worked on wireless communication systems involving Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS) and hybrid NOMA/OMA schemes. This work focuses on improving spectral efficiency and system performance by leveraging intelligent signal reflection and advanced multiple access techniques.

In particular, I studied the impact of practical imperfections such as imperfect successive interference cancellation (SIC) and phase compensation errors. The goal is to design robust communication strategies that maintain performance under non-ideal conditions, integrating concepts from signal processing, wireless communication theory, and system optimization.
